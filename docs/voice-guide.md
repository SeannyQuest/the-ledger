# Daonra Voice Guide

## The Voice: Last Week Tonight meets investigative journalism

Think John Oliver. Righteous anger delivered with wit. Complex issues made accessible through humor, analogies, and "wait, WHAT?" moments. Every piece should make you laugh, then make you furious, then make you want to do something.

## Rules

### Writing Style
- **NO em dashes.** Ever. Use periods, commas, colons, or just start a new sentence.
- **No corporate speak.** No "stakeholders," no "synergies," no "at the end of the day."
- **No AI voice.** No "it's worth noting," no "it's important to understand," no "let's dive in."
- **Short sentences hit harder.** Use them.
- **Lead with the most shocking fact.** Don't bury it.
- **Name names.** Don't say "some politicians." Say "Senator Bob Menendez, who was literally found with gold bars in his jacket."
- **Use concrete comparisons.** Don't say "$886 billion defense budget." Say "The US spends more on its military than the next 10 countries combined. That's like showing up to a neighborhood basketball game in full body armor driving a tank."
- **Swear when appropriate.** Not constantly, but a well-placed "what the actual hell" hits different than "this is concerning."
- **Be funny, not cute.** Humor should sharpen the point, not soften it.

### Sourcing
- **Every claim needs a source.** Period.
- **Link to primary sources** when possible (court documents, government data, SEC filings), not just news articles about them.
- **Date your sources.** "(ProPublica, March 2025)" not just "(ProPublica)"
- **When sources conflict, say so.** "The Pentagon says X. Independent auditors found Y. You decide which one has a $2.5 trillion accounting gap."

### Structure
- **Hook in the first sentence.** If the reader isn't angry or laughing by sentence two, rewrite sentence one.
- **Break up walls of text.** Short paragraphs. Pull quotes. Data callouts.
- **End with action.** Every piece should answer "OK so what do I do about this?" with specific, concrete steps.

### Tone Spectrum
- **Default:** Informed, witty, a little pissed off
- **On corruption:** Controlled fury. Let the facts do the screaming.
- **On human suffering:** Respectful. No jokes about victims. Let their stories speak.
- **On absurdity:** Full Oliver. "This is real. This actually happened. In America. In 2025."
- **On solutions:** Genuine hope without naivety. "Here's what actually works, and here's who's blocking it."

### What We're NOT
- Not partisan. We go after Democrats AND Republicans who sell out the public.
- Not conspiracy theorists. Everything is sourced, documented, provable.
- Not nihilistic. "Everything is broken" without "here's how we fix it" is just depression fuel.
- Not preachy. We're not lecturing. We're showing you what's happening with YOUR money, in YOUR name.

## Example Rewrites

**Bad (AI voice):**
"It's worth noting that the pharmaceutical industry spent approximately $387 million on lobbying efforts in 2024, which represents a significant investment in political influence."

**Good (Daonra voice):**
"The pharmaceutical industry spent $387 million lobbying Congress in 2024. To put that in perspective, that's roughly $724,000 per member of Congress. And somehow, mysteriously, Congress keeps voting against letting Medicare negotiate drug prices. Weird how that works."

**Bad (dry report):**
"Congressional stock trading remains a significant concern, with multiple members of Congress outperforming market indices by substantial margins."

**Good (Daonra voice):**
"Paul Pelosi turned a 950% return between 2012 and 2024. The S&P 500 did 439% in the same period. The penalty for violating the STOCK Act? Two hundred dollars. And it's waivable. Number of members of Congress who have been prosecuted under it: zero. Not one. Ever."

# The Price Tag on Your Democracy Is $15.9 Billion (And Someone Else Already Paid It)

*How five unelected judges turned American elections into a billionaire's yard sale*

---

In 2024, six individual human beings each wrote a check for $100 million or more to influence who runs the United States government. Six people. Not six million. Not six thousand. Six. Meanwhile, 85% of Americans told pollsters that the political system is "a money-driven game only the wealthy can play." That's not cynicism. That's pattern recognition.

And it all traces back to one Supreme Court decision.

## The Day Democracy Got a Price Tag

On January 21, 2010, the Supreme Court decided, in a 5-4 ruling, that corporations have the same First Amendment rights as people and that spending money counts as speech. The case was *Citizens United v. Federal Election Commission*, and it effectively told every billionaire and multinational corporation in America: go nuts.

Justice Anthony Kennedy, writing for the majority alongside Roberts, Scalia, Thomas, and Alito, argued that independent political expenditures "do not lead to corruption or the appearance of corruption." ([Supreme Court opinion, 2010](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/558/310/))

Let that sink in. Five people in robes looked at unlimited corporate spending in elections and said: "Nah, that's fine. No corruption here."

Justice John Paul Stevens wrote a 90-page dissent calling it a threat to "the integrity of elected institutions across the Nation." Ninety pages. That's not a dissent. That's a man screaming into the void with citations.

## The Money Explosion: By the Numbers

Here's what happened after the ruling. Try not to throw your phone.

Total spending on U.S. elections in 2008, the last presidential cycle before Citizens United: **$5.3 billion**. A lot of money. An absurd amount of money, really.

Total spending in 2020: **$14 billion**. ([OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org))

That's not a gradual increase. That's a line on a graph that looks like it's trying to escape the chart. In twelve years, election spending nearly tripled. By 2024, the total hit **$15.9 billion**. To put that in perspective, that's more than the entire GDP of Iceland. We are now spending more money picking our leaders than some countries generate in a year.

And that's just the money we can see.

## Dark Money: The Stuff We Can't See (Which Is the Point)

"Dark money" is political spending where the original source is hidden from the public. It flows through 501(c)(4) "social welfare" organizations, trade associations, and shell LLCs with names so generic they sound like they were generated by a bored intern. These groups don't have to reveal their donors. That's not a bug. That's the entire business model.

In 2010, dark money accounted for about **$105 million**. By 2024, it hit a record **$1.9 billion**. ([Brennan Center for Justice, 2024](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/dark-money-hit-record-high-19-billion-2024-federal-races))

Cumulative dark money since Citizens United: **over $4 billion** in *reported* amounts. The real number is higher. We just don't know how much higher, because that's literally the point of dark money.

Shell companies and undisclosed nonprofits funneled **$1.3 billion** into Super PACs in the 2024 cycle alone. Nobody knows who's behind those shell companies. Could be an American billionaire. Could be a foreign government. Could be both. We have no way to tell, and that's not an accident.

## Super PACs: The "Independent" Spending That Isn't

Citizens United and its companion case *SpeechNow.org v. FEC* created Super PACs. These groups can raise unlimited money and spend unlimited money, as long as they don't "coordinate" with the candidates they support.

The coordination rule is adorable. Like a "please don't feed the bears" sign at a zoo where the bears run the gift shop.

Here's how it works in practice: former staffers and close allies of candidates run their Super PACs. Campaigns post raw video footage online that Super PACs scoop up for ads. Candidates attend Super PAC fundraisers. Single-candidate Super PACs are functionally just extensions of the campaign with extra steps. And the FEC, which is supposed to enforce the rules, is permanently deadlocked 3-3 along partisan lines. It's a regulatory body that can't regulate. It's the DMV of democracy, except at least the DMV eventually processes your paperwork. ([Campaign Legal Center](https://campaignlegal.org))

### Who's Writing the Checks?

The top donors in the 2024 cycle read like a Forbes list with a political agenda:

- **Elon Musk:** $291 million to Republican/Trump PACs, including $118.6 million to America PAC
- **Timothy Mellon:** $197 million, with $150 million going to MAGA Inc.
- **Richard and Elizabeth Uihlein:** $139 million to Restoration PAC and MAGA Inc.
- **Miriam Adelson:** $105.8 million to Preserve America
- **Kenneth Griffin:** $100 million+ to Republican causes
- **Jeff and Janine Yass:** $100 million+ to Republican causes

([OpenSecrets, 2024](https://www.opensecrets.org); [Brennan Center, 2024](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/megadonors-playing-larger-role-presidential-race-fec-data-shows))

The top 50 donors collectively gave **$2.5 billion**. Over 10% of all Super PAC money came from just four donors.

This is what "one person, one vote" looks like when one person's "speech" is worth $291 million and yours is worth whatever you can scrape together after rent.

## What They're Buying: The Corporate Capture Report Card

This isn't abstract. The money buys specific outcomes. Let's go industry by industry.

### Big Pharma: $387 Million Well Spent

The pharmaceutical industry spent **$387 million** lobbying Congress in 2024. That's roughly $724,000 per member of Congress. ([OpenSecrets, 2024](https://www.opensecrets.org))

The return on investment? Americans pay the **highest drug prices on Earth**. Merck's Keytruda costs **$191,000 per year** in the U.S. In Germany, it's $89,000. Eliquis costs **$7,100** here. In Japan? **$900**. Same drug. Same molecule. Different lobbyists. ([Senate HELP Committee Report](https://www.help.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/big_pharmas_business_model_report.pdf))

For decades, the industry blocked Medicare from negotiating drug prices. Congress only partially addressed this in 2022, and only for 10 drugs. Ten. Out of thousands. That's not reform. That's a rounding error with good PR.

### Fossil Fuels: Lighting Money on Fire (Also the Planet)

The fossil fuel industry spent **$243 million** lobbying Congress in 2023-2024, and contributed **$96 million** to Trump's 2024 campaign and affiliated PACs. ([OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org))

What'd they get? An estimated **$20 billion per year** in direct subsidies and tax benefits. That's a 200-to-1 return on their lobbying investment. If your stock portfolio performed like fossil fuel lobbying, you'd retire tomorrow.

### Big Tech: Killing Laws Is Cheaper Than Following Them

Google, Amazon, Apple, and Meta combined spent **$95 million** in 2021-2022 specifically to kill antitrust bills. The American Innovation and Choice Online Act had bipartisan support. Voters wanted it. It died anyway. Meta alone dropped **$8 million in a single quarter** in 2025 ahead of its FTC antitrust trial. ([OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org))

### The Grand Total

Federal lobbying hit a record **$4.4 billion** in 2024. There are now **13,000+ registered federal lobbyists**. That's about 22 lobbyists for every single member of Congress. But the real number of people doing lobbying work is estimated at around **100,000**, which works out to roughly 187 influence-peddlers per legislator. ([OpenSecrets, 2024](https://www.opensecrets.org))

Your representative has 187 people whispering in one ear. You have an email form on a website that may or may not work.

## The Revolving Door: Where Lawmakers Go to Cash In

Here's the career path for a modern American legislator:

1. Get elected
2. Vote on things
3. Leave office
4. Get paid by the people you used to vote on things for

In the 1970s, less than **5% of retiring legislators** became lobbyists. By 1998-2004, that number was **43%**. For senators specifically, it was **50%**. Half of all departing senators walked straight into lobbying. ([Public Citizen](https://www.citizen.org); [Center for Public Integrity](https://publicintegrity.org))

A 2019 study found that **59% of representatives** who leave Congress for the private sector join lobbying or consulting firms. ([New America](https://www.newamerica.org))

Some highlights from the Hall of Shame:

- **Bob Livingston (R-LA):** Left Congress in 1999. His lobbying firm pulled in $4.8 million by year two.
- **Eric Cantor (R-VA):** House Majority Leader to Managing Director at Moelis & Co. investment bank. That's not a career change. That's a promotion.
- **Lamar Smith (R-TX):** Advocated for an Alaska mine while in Congress, then left to lobby for the same mine at Akin Gump. He didn't even change the subject.
- **Henry Waxman (D-CA):** Chaired the Energy and Commerce Committee, then went to work at his son's lobbying firm. Keep it in the family.

The "cooling-off period" before former lawmakers can lobby is one year for House members and two years for senators. But they can still develop lobbying strategies, supervise other lobbyists, and register as lobbyists during that period. Many just call themselves "strategic advisers" and do the exact same thing. It's a speed bump on a highway. ([The Fulcrum](https://thefulcrum.us))

## Foreign Money: The Loophole Nobody Can Close

Federal law prohibits foreign nationals from spending money in U.S. elections. Sounds solid. Here's the problem: U.S.-incorporated subsidiaries of 100% foreign-owned corporations can make unlimited political expenditures. Shell LLCs can funnel money to Super PACs with zero requirement to prove the funds aren't foreign. And 501(c)(4) organizations can receive foreign donations and then spend on elections without revealing the source. ([Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org/topics/money-politics/foreign-spending); [Center for American Progress](https://www.americanprogress.org/article/citizens-united-paves-way-foreign-governments-influence-u-s-elections/))

With $1.3 billion flowing through shell companies and dark nonprofits in 2024 alone, it is literally impossible to determine how much foreign money is in the system. Not difficult. Impossible.

Justice Stevens warned about this exact scenario in his dissent. The majority's framework provides no mechanism to prevent foreign corporations from using U.S. subsidiaries to influence American elections. They were warned. They didn't care.

## The Ohio Test Case: What State-Level Corruption Looks Like

If you want to see what dark money does at the state level, look at Ohio.

Energy company **FirstEnergy** funneled **$60 million** through a dark money group linked to Ohio House Speaker **Larry Householder**. The money secured a **$1 billion bailout** for failing nuclear power plants. Householder was convicted in a federal racketeering case. A federal judge called dark money a "breeding ground for corruption." ([Common Cause Ohio](https://www.commoncause.org/ohio/); [Citizens for Ethics](https://www.citizensforethics.org))

This wasn't subtle. A corporation paid $60 million to a politician through an untraceable channel and got a billion-dollar bailout in return. That's a 1,567% return. And the only reason we know about it is because it was so brazen that federal prosecutors could build a racketeering case.

Now think about all the ones that weren't that brazen.

## What Every Other Democracy Does (Spoiler: Not This)

Canada banned corporate donations entirely in 2007. France banned them in 1995. The UK caps total party spending at about £35.1 million. The U.S. spent **$15.9 billion** in 2024. ([Electoral Commission UK](https://www.electoralcommission.org.uk); [Elections Canada](https://www.elections.ca); [International IDEA](https://www.idea.int))

If you applied UK-style spending limits to American elections, total spending would drop by roughly **99%**.

The United States is the only major democracy that allows unlimited anonymous spending to influence elections. We're not the exception. We're the cautionary tale.

## Every Reform Has Been Killed

The DISCLOSE Act, which would simply require Super PACs and dark money groups to reveal donors who give more than $10,000, was first introduced in 2010. It passed the House. It was filibustered in the Senate. It has been reintroduced in every Congress since then. Blocked every single time. ([Brennan Center](https://www.brennancenter.org))

The For the People Act, which would have expanded voting rights, reformed campaign finance, and ended gerrymandering, passed the House in 2019 and 2021. Killed by Senate filibuster both times.

Multiple constitutional amendments have been proposed to overturn Citizens United. None have come close to the two-thirds supermajority needed.

Who blocks reform? Senate Republicans filibuster every major campaign finance bill. The U.S. Chamber of Commerce lobbies against disclosure requirements. The Koch network's Americans for Prosperity campaigns against donor transparency. The FEC can't enforce existing rules because it's permanently deadlocked. And the Supreme Court's conservative majority continues to strike down campaign finance regulations.

**82% of Americans** say money in politics threatens democracy. **77%** support a constitutional amendment to fix it. **83% of Republicans** say big donors have too much influence. **80% of Democrats** say the same thing. ([Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org))

This is one of the most bipartisan issues in America. And nothing happens. Because the people who would have to fix it are the same people benefiting from it.

## What You Can Actually Do About It

This is the part where we don't just leave you angry. Here's what works.

### Support the Organizations Fighting This

- [**Brennan Center for Justice**](https://www.brennancenter.org) tracks dark money and litigates for reform
- [**Campaign Legal Center**](https://campaignlegal.org) files legal challenges against dark money abuse
- [**American Promise**](https://americanpromise.net) is building the movement for a constitutional amendment
- [**Issue One**](https://issueone.org) works on bipartisan anti-corruption reforms
- [**Represent Us**](https://represent.us) passes anti-corruption laws at the state and local level

### Push for State-Level Reform

22 states and 800+ municipalities have already called for a constitutional amendment. Your state can be next. State legislatures can pass disclosure laws, public financing systems, and anti-corruption measures that the federal government refuses to touch. Arizona and Oregon have already enacted dark money disclosure laws. ([American Promise](https://americanpromise.net))

### Contact Your Representatives

Yes, really. Tell your senators to support the DISCLOSE Act. Tell them to support the For the People Act. Tell them you support a constitutional amendment to overturn Citizens United. **82% of Americans agree with you.** Make them hear it.

Find your representatives: [**congress.gov/members**](https://www.congress.gov/members)

### Follow the Money

Use [**OpenSecrets.org**](https://www.opensecrets.org) to look up who funds your representatives. Use [**FollowTheMoney.org**](https://www.followthemoney.org) for state-level races. Knowledge is the first step. Sharing it is the second.

### Vote in Every Election

Not just the presidential ones. State legislatures, city councils, judicial races, school boards. Dark money targets these races precisely because turnout is low and attention is elsewhere. Show up.

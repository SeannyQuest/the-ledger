## The STOCK Act: A Law Designed to Fail

The Stop Trading on Congressional Knowledge Act was supposed to be the fix. Passed in 2012 with bipartisan fanfare and press conferences. Members of Congress should not profit from classified information! Obviously! The American people demand accountability!

Then they wrote the law with the enforcement power of a sternly worded Post-it note.

Here's how it works: members must disclose stock trades within 45 days. If they don't, they get a $200 fine. If they ask nicely, the Ethics Committee can waive that fine. There is no independent enforcement mechanism. Congress polices itself.

That's like asking a fox to audit the henhouse and then giving the fox a key to the padlock. *(OpenSecrets, 2024)*

In 2024, his portfolio returned roughly 71%. The S&P 500 returned 25%. In 2023, he hit 66%. The S&P did about 25%. His five-year annualized return sits around 22.8% per year. The market averaged 12%.

Sure. And I should be able to "participate" in the poker game where I can see everyone else's cards.

### Richard Burr: The COVID Cash-Out King

Richard Burr was the Chairman of the Senate Intelligence Committee during early COVID-19 briefings. Those briefings were classified. They contained information about just how bad the pandemic was going to get, weeks before the public knew.

On February 13, 2020, Burr sold between $628,000 and $1.7 million in stocks across 33 transactions. He specifically dumped hotel and airline stocks. You know, the sectors that were about to get obliterated by a global pandemic that he had classified intelligence about.

The DOJ investigated. No charges filed. Case closed. *(Forbes, 2020; Wikipedia)*

Just imagine, for one second, if a regular person at a pharmaceutical company sold their stock before a bad earnings report based on information they got from an internal meeting. That person would go to prison. It's called insider trading. Martha Stewart went to prison for it. But when a sitting senator does it with classified pandemic intelligence? Investigation closed. Nothing to see here.

### Tommy Tuberville: 130 Trades, Zero Consequences

Tommy Tuberville, Republican senator from Alabama and former college football coach, failed to disclose approximately 130 separate stock trades made between January and May of 2021. The total undisclosed transaction value: somewhere between $894,000 and $3.56 million.

Some of those trades? Healthcare companies. While he was sitting on the Senate Health Committee.

His punishment: he paid the $200 fines. Then he kept trading. *(Business Insider, 2022; Campaign Legal Center)*

## The Lobbying Machine

Now let's talk about how industries buy the laws they want, because the stock trading is really just the dessert. The main course is lobbying.

Federal lobbying spending hit **$5.24 billion** in 2025. That's a $768 million increase from 2024, the largest year-over-year jump ever recorded. *(LegiStorm, 2025)*

There are 535 members of Congress. That works out to roughly **$9.8 million in lobbying spending per member of Congress per year**.

Think about that. For every single person you elected to represent you, industries are spending $9.8 million per year to make sure those people represent THEM instead.

The top spenders:

- **Pharmaceuticals and health:** $388 million in 2024, on pace to blow past that in 2025. PhRMA, the industry's main lobbying group, spent $37.9 million in 2025 alone.
- **Defense:** $293.3 million in 2025, an all-time high, employing 950 lobbyists.
- **Tech:** Surging, driven by AI regulation panic.
- **Finance and insurance:** The U.S. Chamber of Commerce alone spent $76.2 million in 2024.

*(Bloomberg Government, 2025; OpenSecrets; Quorum)*

There are approximately 12,000 registered federal lobbyists. That's 22 lobbyists for every single member of Congress. Your representative has 22 people whose full-time job is to convince them to do what corporations want. How many people does your neighborhood have doing that job for you? I'm guessing the number is lower than 22.

## Dark Money: When Regular Corruption Isn't Enough

Campaign contributions take the system to its logical conclusion. While direct corporate contributions to candidates are technically banned, the system is built entirely on workarounds:

- Super PACs spent **$2.7 billion** in the 2024 election cycle. *(Brennan Center, 2025)*
- Dark money hit a record **$1.9 billion** in 2024, nearly doubling the prior record of $1 billion in 2020.
- Total 2024 federal election spending: at least **$15.9 billion**. *(OpenSecrets)*

All of this was made possible by **Citizens United v. FEC** (2010), the Supreme Court decision that declared corporate spending on elections is protected "free speech." Combined with McCutcheon v. FEC (2014), which struck down aggregate contribution limits, these decisions essentially deregulated political bribery.

The result: $15.9 billion spent on a single election cycle. That's not democracy. That's an auction.

## The Revolving Door: Where Lobbyists Become Lawmakers Become Lobbyists

Here's the final piece. The revolving door isn't a metaphor. It's a business model.

Lobbyists get hired as congressional staffers. They learn the system from the inside. They build relationships with members. Then they leave and go back to lobbying, now armed with insider knowledge, personal connections, and the ability to draft legislation their corporate clients want.

Or it works the other way: members of Congress serve for a few terms, build their network, then retire into lobbying careers where they make five to ten times their government salary.

The knowledge of how government works IS the product being sold.

For comparison, here's what other countries do:

- **Canada:** 5-year lobbying ban for senior government officials after they leave.
- **France:** 3-year cooling-off period before officials can join the industries they regulated.
- **United States:** 1 to 2 year cooling-off period, widely circumvented through "strategic advisory" roles that technically aren't lobbying.

One to two years. And that's the rule they mostly ignore anyway.

## The Numbers in Context

A 2004 Georgia State University study published in the Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis found that senators' stock portfolios outperformed the market by 12% annually. Websites like Capitol Trades and Quiver Quantitative now track congressional stock trades in real time. People literally copy what members of Congress buy and sell because they know those trades are based on information the rest of us don't have.

We have created a system where:

1. Industries spend $5.24 billion per year lobbying Congress to write favorable laws.
2. Those same industries pour $15.9 billion into elections to pick the people writing those laws.
3. The people writing those laws trade stocks based on what they learn in classified briefings.
4. The penalty for getting caught is $200, waivable, with zero criminal enforcement.
5. When they leave Congress, they become lobbyists, completing the circle.

This isn't a bug. It's the feature.

## So What Do You Do About It?

You're probably angry now. Good. Here's what you can actually do:

**1. Support the ETHICS Act and the Ban Congressional Stock Trading Act.** Multiple versions have been introduced. None have passed. Call your representative and ask them why. If they can't give you a straight answer, you have your answer.

**2. Track your representatives' trades.** Sites like [Capitol Trades](https://www.capitoltrades.com/) and [Quiver Quantitative](https://www.quiverquant.com/congresstrading/) make this free and searchable. See what they're buying. See what legislation they're voting on. Connect the dots yourself.

**3. Support campaign finance reform.** Organizations like the [Brennan Center for Justice](https://www.brennancenter.org/) and [OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org/) are pushing for transparency and limits on dark money. They need public support to overcome $5.24 billion worth of opposition.

**4. Vote in primaries.** General elections get all the attention, but primaries are where incumbents are most vulnerable. If your representative has been in office for 20 years and their stock portfolio looks like a hedge fund, maybe it's time for someone new.

**5. Talk about it.** The system survives because most people don't know how it works. Share this article. Bring it up at dinner. Make it weird. Because it IS weird that we let this happen.

The system isn't going to reform itself. The people profiting from it have $5.24 billion reasons to keep it exactly the way it is.

It's going to take the rest of us.
